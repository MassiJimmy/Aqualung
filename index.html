WHY ARE YOUR
GOGGLES FOGGING?

Fog is a common challenge for all swimmers. It's the result of condensation. Your swim goggles cool down in the water, while the area around your eyes heats up due to rising body temperature stimulated by the effort. This unbalance causes water droplets to form on the inside of your goggles, which creates fog.

All our swimming goggles feature a hydrophilic anti-fog treatment with ingredients absorbing moisture. It allows to attract the vapor droplet and spread them into a thin layer spread over the surface for a greater visibility while swimming

while all our swimming goggles feature anti-fog treatment, it can still be damaged by lack of caring, wear and tear.

HOW TO TAKE CARE OF YOUR SWIM GOGGLES?

Our care guide shares tips to PREVENT damage prior your swim, to PRESERVE your goggles post swimmng and a monthly tutorial for a long-term PROTECTION

Keep your goggles fog free and in top condition!

PRIOR TO SWIMMING
PREVENT >>>
pool

PUT EVERYWEAR ON DRY

Avoid having water already inside the goggles. Water will warm up in contact with your skin and increase fogging.

pool

DON'T SPIT ON THE LENS OR PUT EYEWEAR IN WATER

Keeping the inner lens dry enables the antifog to work better and last longer.

pool

DON'T TOUCH OR RUB THE INSIDE OF THE LENS

It would damage the inner lens and the antifog treatment applied on the inside of the lens. Also, the dirt and oil from your fingers can reduce your visibility while swimming.

POST SWIMMING
PRESERVE >>>
pool

RINCE WITH FRESH WATER

Remove chlorine or salt which can eat into your swimming goggle material and make the strap and gasket go stiff and brittle.
